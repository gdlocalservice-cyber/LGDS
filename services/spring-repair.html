What’s going on

  • Door won’t open or only lifts a few inches
  • Loud bang from the garage (spring may have snapped)
  • Door feels very heavy or drops quickly
  • Gaps in a torsion spring or a hanging extension spring

Tip: If the door feels extremely heavy or won’t open more than a few inches, stop using it and call - garage door springs can be dangerous.

How we fix it

  • Confirm spring type and door weight
  • Replace spring(s) with the correct, cycle-rated springs
  • Set proper balance and tension
  • Inspect cables, drums, bearings, and safety systems

We focus on safe, durable repairs - no guesswork and no shortcuts.

What to know

Springs are under extreme tension. DIY spring work is one of the most dangerous garage door repairs. We use the right tools and safety procedures to replace and balance springs correctly.

If your door uses two torsion springs, yes - replace both. When one breaks, the other has completed the same number of cycles and is likely to fail within weeks or months. Replacing both at once saves you a second service call and keeps the door balanced.
